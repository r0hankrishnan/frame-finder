Overview

Pre-strung for extra savings! 


One of the most popular selling Hyper Carbon models. Although light and maneuverable with a slight head heavy balance, the 5.3 Oversize is not as top-heavy as most other Hammer models. At 27.5 inches, it provides a little more leverage and reach than a standard length racquet, without impeding reaction to balls hit close to the body. Doubles players may favor the Oversize because of the the power provided on serve with plenty of pop and forgiveness at net. Recommended to beginner and intermediate players.

The customer comments shown below were gathered using our previous feedback system. While these comments are not used in the calculation of the star rating for this product, they are displayed to help provide you with the most complete customer feedback available.

Comments: I just bought my third Wilson Hyper Hammer 5.3 Stretch OS, so I figured it's time to give my two cents on why it's such a well-designed racquet, with appeal to an unusually broad spectrum of players. I picked this up while getting back into tennis after a 14-year break. I have moved from 3.5 to 4.5 in about a year, and this racquet has been with me every step of the way. In short, whatever you need this racquet to be, it can be. Are you an older doubles club player who has lost a step and wants a light racquet with a big sweetspot? Great, string it up loose with an arm-friendly multi and that's what you've got. Are you a baseline basher with long, full strokes? Put in a tight poly and the 5.3 still gives you that large sweetspot with unusual precision for such a light, oversized frame. The head-heavy balance makes the 5.3 feel MUCH heftier than it's feather-light 9.0oz strung swing weight. Many times, I've picked up my partners racquet and am shocked that it's actually lighter than the Hammer. That's not a bad thing. Usually a racquet this light would be disqualified for heavy hitting much beyond the 4.0 range, but the head-heavy balance keeps it from getting pushed around in your hand. On the serve, it gives you great momentum to the point I have a hard time serving with head-light racquets at this point. The combination of extreme lightness and a fairly open string pattern make this racquet a delight for anyone that likes wrist-flicking, angles, etc. That extended half inch really matters, and it's so much fun pulling off some low-percentage shots with this thing when you're on the dead run. I'm a 100% two-handed backhand player, but this racquet is so light I can actually make some effective shots with one hand when I'm on my heels. The Hyper Hammer 5.3 is VERY stiff, which can be a plus or a minus depending on your preferences. There's nothing buttery about this racquet, and frankly, it doesn't have a ton of feel. It's stiff and harsh, and the vibrations are substantial. I think a double-work shockbuster is a must for the Hyper Hammer 5.3. That said, if you like that stiff, crisp feel, it's a joy to hit. I actually leaned into a full bed of poly strings like Wilson Revolve, and I think that contrast of the powerful frame with low-powered strings really brings the Hyper Hammer 5.3 to life. The vibration/stiffness never caused me any serious arm problems, and I play nearly 7 days/week; however, I use an eastern forehand (a very elbow-friendly grip), so I can't speak to how this stick would effect someone with a more modern swing. The only drawback I can think of is the Hyper Hammer 5.3 sometimes feels unstable on volleys when blocking back a hard shot, unless you really take the ball dead-on in the sweet spot. That might just be my bad technique. I pretty much just come to the net to shake hands (or tap racquets), so this is a nearly irrelevant issue for me. Throw in the price, and you've got a total winner. I recently demoed the Wilson Blade 104 v7, and it felt okay, but there's no reason I'd buy that when the Hammer 5.3 is literally half the price and does a lot of things better (and in my opinion nothing worse). I'm actually surprised from a marketing perspective that Wilson still sells these. The sales must be spectacular (in constant production since 1999!), since they clearly decided it's better to keep selling the frames than trying to get 5.3 users to transition into the newer lines. Give this classic a try. It's one of a kind. 4.5 baselineWilson Revolve Spin 17g @ 57lbs
